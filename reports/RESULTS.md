# Results — next-window volatility prediction

Held-out **test set** evaluation (the most recent 15% of windows, never seen in
training or validation). Generated by `make evaluate` (`ml/evaluate.py`), which
scores the trained LSTM and the naive baselines on the **identical** test
sequences and writes `models/eval_metrics.json` plus the plots in this folder.

## Setup

- **Data:** historical 5-minute BTC/USDT, 2022-01-01 → 2026-03-14 — 441,465
  sequences of 24 windows × 11 features.
- **Split:** chronological 70 / 15 / 15 (train / val / test). Test size = 66,219
  windows. No shuffling across the time boundary.
- **Model:** attention-pooled 2-layer LSTM, AdamW + `ReduceLROnPlateau`, gradient
  clipping, early stopping (best val at epoch 16, stopped at 22). Trained on CPU;
  the GPU run on Kaggle (`kaggle/`, 60 epochs, bf16) is expected to improve on
  this.
- **Target:** the *next* window's volatility = (high − low) / open.

## Test-set comparison

| method            |     RMSE |      MAE |     R²  | sMAPE% | DirAcc |
|-------------------|---------:|---------:|--------:|-------:|-------:|
| **LSTM**          | 0.001111 | 0.000655 | **0.517** | 44.0 | 0.676 |
| persistence       | 0.001281 | 0.000764 | 0.358   | 48.6 | 0.000 |
| EWMA              | 0.001106 | 0.000633 | 0.521   | 40.6 | 0.690 |
| rolling_mean(6)   | 0.001153 | 0.000652 | 0.480   | 41.4 | 0.690 |
| rolling_mean(12)  | 0.001187 | 0.000661 | 0.449   | 41.7 | 0.692 |

`DirAcc` = directional accuracy: did the prediction correctly call volatility
going **up vs down** relative to the last observed window? Persistence is ~0 by
construction (it predicts exactly the last value, so its predicted change is
always zero).

## Reading the result

- The trained LSTM **clearly beats** the persistence and rolling-mean baselines
  on every metric (RMSE, MAE, R², directional accuracy).
- It is **statistically tied with EWMA**, the strongest classical baseline —
  expected, because volatility is highly autocorrelated and EWMA is a very strong
  prior for it. Matching a tuned EWMA with a learned model that *also* ingests
  order-flow, open interest, funding, and book-depth features is a solid,
  honest result, and leaves clear headroom: the full GPU training run (more
  epochs, larger batches, bf16) and richer sentiment at serving time are the
  levers to pull ahead.
- Crucially, the comparison is **leakage-free and reproducible**: the same
  chronological split fractions are stored in the model checkpoint and re-used by
  the evaluator, and the baselines see only in-sequence history.
